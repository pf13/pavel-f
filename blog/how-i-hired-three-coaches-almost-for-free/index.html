How I Hired Three Coaches Almost for Free

Most AI content focuses on productivity or business. That's not surprising, given that even households with high internet usage and Amazon Prime rarely use AI tools. If you're mostly scrolling LinkedIn, you might not notice how few people know that prompt quality does matter.

Improving as a cyclist is straightforward: it takes focused training, strength work, and good nutrition.

Back then, it used to be expensive and difficult to manage everything. One coach would handle cycling, and another would handle your gym work. You might use apps to track nutrition and activities, and spend hours searching Reddit or Quora for insights. Or you could just ride for fun ‚Äî which, honestly, might be the best option if that's enough for you. :)

Things have changed dramatically. It's still not a revolution, but it's a big leap compared to the reality we had two years ago.

Let's Set It Up

Here is my approach to being better at cycling ‚Äî one that could help you in any sport. I have three coaches: one for cycling plans, one for nutrition, and another one for strength and conditioning.

The first coach uses my data from Garmin and Whoop to analyze my current conditioning, recent activities, recovery, and prepares a weekly cycling workout plan.

The second coach uses photos and simple notes to calculate my daily protein and carb intake, syncing them with the first coach.

The third coach reviews the outputs from the first two coaches, prepares a general conditioning program for the gym, and works on my injuries and physical problems.

Guess ‚Äî how many of them are real people?

The answer is simple ‚Äî just one: the third coach.

I hope it's only a matter of time before all your favorite sports apps stop delivering simple activity aggregation with absolutely useless insights like 'You've done a great job.' ChatGPT can already read your data ‚Äî even from screenshots ‚Äî and give you meaningful, structured feedback.

The process

How I'm doing this ‚Äî For the first two, I wrote two big prompts for separate projects in ChatGPT. DM me if you would like some prompt examples. Each started as a single chat that I can use for over a month without restarting.

Then, I manually align them and allow them to read each other's recommendations and my data. Here is another call to action for all sports app developers.

Then the real coach adjusts my workload and prepares injury-specific training. Here we go ‚Äì one more use-case for sport apps.

It works relatively well with limited interruptions. ChatGPT can easily replace your favorite (and boring) calorie tracker ‚Äî you don't have to do anything, just chat, speak, or send photos of your meals to calculate your daily intake and receive a human-like message such as "it would be better to add some protein by the end of the day for better recovery." It works with very high accuracy ‚Äì just try it.

ChatGPT can easily analyze your data from Garmin or Strava and provide much better insights on structuring your weekly/monthly activities, rather than paying for Strava Premium, etc.

The real coach can take a look with a certain amount of skepticism, slightly adjust everything, and delete irrelevant things.

While it may sound complex, this setup is far more straightforward ‚Äî and cheaper ‚Äî than hiring three human experts or configuring clunky fitness apps. Anyway, you're probably already paying for the ChatGPT.
